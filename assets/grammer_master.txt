*** RBSE CLASS 12 ENGLISH GRAMMAR (8 MARKS) ***

=== TOPIC 1: CLAUSES (2 Marks) ===
DEFINITION: Joining two sentences using a connector (who, whose, whom, which, that, when, where, why).

RULE 1: 'WHO' (Person - Subject)
- Use when the blank is after a person and before a verb.
- Example: The boy ___ is standing there is my brother. (Answer: who)
- TRICK: Person + [WHO] + Verb.

RULE 2: 'WHOM' (Person - Object)
- Use when the blank is after a person and before another Subject + Verb.
- Example: The man ___ I met yesterday was a doctor. (Answer: whom)
- TRICK: Person + [WHOM] + Subject + Verb.

RULE 3: 'WHOSE' (Possession/Owner)
- Use when the blank is between two nouns (Person + Thing/Relation).
- Example: The man ___ shirt is blue is my uncle. (Answer: whose)
- TRICK: Person + [WHOSE] + Thing/Person related.

RULE 4: 'WHICH' (Things/Animals)
- Use for non-living things or animals.
- Example: The dog ___ bit me is mad. (Answer: which)
- Example: The car ___ I bought is red. (Answer: which)

RULE 5: 'WHERE' (Place)
- Use when referring to a place.
- Example: This is the school ___ I studied. (Answer: where)

=== TOPIC 2: SYNTHESIS (2 Marks) ===
DEFINITION: Combining two sentences into one (Simple, Complex, or Compound).

TYPE A: COMBINE INTO SIMPLE SENTENCE
1. Using Participles (ing):
   - Q: He saw a tiger. He ran away.
   - A: Seeing a tiger, he ran away.
2. Using Infinitives (to):
   - Q: He went to Agra. He wanted to see the Taj.
   - A: He went to Agra to see the Taj.
3. Using 'Too...to':
   - Q: He is very weak. He cannot walk.
   - A: He is too weak to walk.

TYPE B: COMBINE INTO COMPLEX SENTENCE (use if/because/although/who/which)
1. Condition:
   - Q: Work hard. You will pass.
   - A: If you work hard, you will pass.
2. Reason:
   - Q: He did not come. He was ill.
   - A: He did not come because he was ill.
3. Contrast:
   - Q: He is rich. He is unhappy.
   - A: Although he is rich, he is unhappy.

TYPE C: COMBINE INTO COMPOUND SENTENCE (use and/but/so/or/not only...but also)
- Q: He is intelligent. He is diligent.
- A: He is not only intelligent but also diligent.

=== TOPIC 3: PHRASAL VERBS (2 Marks) ===
(Strictly memorize these 7 verbs for RBSE)

1. BREAK (To divide/destroy)
   - Break down: Stop working (machinery) or Start crying (emotion).
     * Ex: The car broke down.
   - Break into: Enter forcibly (theft).
     * Ex: The thief broke into the house.
   - Break out: Spread suddenly (war/fire/disease).
     * Ex: Corona broke out in 2019.
   - Break with: End a relationship/quarrel.

2. BRING (To carry)
   - Bring up: Rear/Educate a child.
     * Ex: She was brought up by her aunt.
   - Bring out: Publish/Highlight.
     * Ex: He brought out a new book.
   - Bring about: Cause to happen.

3. CARRY (To bear)
   - Carry on: Continue.
     * Ex: Carry on your work.
   - Carry out: Execute/Obey orders.
     * Ex: Soldiers carry out orders.

4. COME (To arrive)
   - Come across: Meet by chance.
     * Ex: I came across an old friend.
   - Come off: Take place/occur.
     * Ex: The ceremony comes off tomorrow.

5. GET (To receive)
   - Get over: Recover (from illness/shock).
     * Ex: He got over his illness.
   - Get along: Be friendly/Manage.
     * Ex: They get along well.
   - Get up: Rise from bed.

6. PUT (To place)
   - Put on: Wear.
     * Ex: Put on your shoes.
   - Put out: Extinguish (fire/light).
     * Ex: Put out the lamp.
   - Put off: Postpone.
     * Ex: The meeting was put off.
   - Put up with: Tolerate/Bear.
     * Ex: I cannot put up with this insult.

7. TURN (To rotate)
   - Turn down: Reject/Refuse.
     * Ex: She turned down my offer.
   - Turn out: Prove to be / Expel.
     * Ex: He turned out to be a thief.

=== TOPIC 4: PREPOSITIONS (2 Marks) ===
Common Exam Prepositions:
1. AT: Exact time (at 5 PM), small places (at Ramnagar).
2. IN: Months/Years (in 2024), big places (in Jaipur).
3. ON: Days/Dates (on Monday, on 15th August).
4. SINCE: Point of time (since Morning, since 1947).
5. FOR: Period of time (for 2 hours, for 3 days).
6. BETWEEN: Two people/things.
7. AMONG: More than two.
8. BESIDE: By the side of.
9. BESIDES: In addition to.
